About Me

Hi. I’m a communication design, graphic designer.
From project to project, I want to be able to capture a brands primary message within my design and take a strict approach to a colors, compositions, and type working together.
Using Adobe programs such as Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign and others, I'm comfortable with creating highly polished designs for digital and print, and can do primary and secondary physical work, enabling a three dimensional look.

Services

UI / UX Design

I develop interfaces that blend functionality and aesthetics through user-centered UX/UI design. Based on UX research, I design intuitive user flows, visualize prototypes in Figma and Adobe XD, and iterate based on feedback. UI design utilizes responsive layout, typography, micro-interactions, and more to enhance informativeness and ease of use. A consistent UI style guide supports collaboration and aligns with project goals and user characteristics.

Visual Identity

I focus on creating a cohesive and powerful expression of a brand's identity through branding and brand experience (BX) design. Through research, I develop a deep understanding of a brand's core message and the needs of its target audience. Then,I consider the critical touchpoints between a brand and its customers to design intuitive user flows that help users experience and understand the brand's value naturally.

Interaction Design

My strength is creating intuitive and effective communication between users and brands through interaction design. I design with subtle interaction elements and user feedback to ensure that at each touchpoint, users understand the brand's message and connect with it naturally. We do this through iterations of prototyping and user testing, making adjustments to optimize the experience for real users.

Projects

Project 1

"A Better Choice" is a fictional bookstore brand created to inspire better choices through reading. The design emphasizes books' power to guide people toward happier, more fulfilling lives, imparting wisdom and fostering personal growth through thoughtful selections and engaging reads.

Project 2

"Chococo" is a fictional gourmet chocolate brand rooted in deep tradition. Its branding features a charming, chocolate-loving baby angel as the iconic character, along with a cacao bean incorporated into the logo. This design evokes a craving for chocolate, making Chococo the perfect choice for gifting.

Project 3

This project involves designing a poster to encourage families to go camping in the fall. Camping means enjoying early autumn nights outdoors, with wooden poles and the forest as a roof. The poster aims to convey the message of taking a break from daily hassles through an illustration.

Project 4

This campaign project aims to show appreciation to mothers on “Mother's Day,” a day to recognize their hard work and express gratitude. The design honors Mother’s Day by celebrating and thanking mothers for their love, dedication, and countless sacrifices, emphasizing their irreplaceable role in our lives.

Project 5

“Change up” is a branding project encouraging exercise as more than just dieting but as a way to rejuvenate life through balance and regular activity to feel and look your best. The logo features a muscular arm lifting dumbbells, with heart-shaped weights symbolizing the brand’s motto of enjoying exercise.

Project 6

This project involves designing an app to improve sleep quality by enhancing your "Danjam" and tracking sleep duration. The "Danjam" app allows you to easily listen to, measure, and monitor snoring patterns, helping you understand them better and find effective solutions for a good night's sleep.

Project 7

This diorama project, titled "Want to go skating at Merlion Kacang?" reimagines Singapore’s iconic Merlion escaping the intense heat to relax at the serene ice rink, Kacang. Two figure skaters glide gracefully across ice scoops, inviting spectators to join this unique winter spectacle and creating a memorable seasonal experience.

Project 8

This illustration project is for those who lack time to enjoy the scenery outside. Inspired by busy subway riders focused on their phones, I imagined a train traveling through the sea, with humpback whales visible through the windows. Using pencils, I focused on capturing the passengers' tired expressions in detail.

Project 9

This photography project captures a Chinese temple in Singapore’s Raffles Place, the central business district where modern history began. Stamford Raffles and the British East India Company landed here to establish a free port. I aimed to preserve this historic location's essence and its warmth.
